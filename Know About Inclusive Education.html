Inclusive Education In Vanuatu




Brief History

Vanuatu Formely New Hebrides, Is An Archipelago Forming A "Y" Shape Stretching From Hiu Island From The North To Aneityum In The South With A Total Land Of 12,336 Square Kilometers.

Vanuatu Has A Population Of Approximately 300,000 People According To 2020 National Census. On The 30th July 1980, Vanuatu Achieved Independence From France And The United Kingdom. Vanuatu Has Abolished Most Of The Colonial Dual System But It Still Using The Mediums Of Instructions In It's Education System (French And English Languages).

The National Disability Policy And Plan Of Action 2008 - 2015 Was Finally Approved And In 2016, At The Begining Of The School Year, The Inclusive Education Classroom Was Opened At Freshwater Bilingual School With The First Ever Government Inclusive Education Teacher, Mrs Janet Ure Bongmassing.

The Inclusive Education Class Started With Eighteen (18) Students In Which Seventeen (17) Were In The Main Stream Classes And One (1) Was A Full Time Student Who Came Straight From Home To Inclusive Education Class.

The Inclusive Meetings And Trainings Were Conducted By The National Inclusive Coordinator, The Inclusive Officer At VESSP. Later The University Of MONASH Conducted A Training On Inclusion In Schools And Communities Including Participants From Non Government Organisations (NGO's) And The Government. More Awarenesses On Inclusive Education Policy Were Rolled Out Starting From 2016 To 2020 Both In Urbans And The Rural Areas In Vanuatu.

Now It's Been Six Years Since The Establishment Of Inclusive Education In Vanuatu And However, There Are A Lot Of Children Living Disabilities That Needs To Be Addressed.

Special Thank You To Australia Pacific Technical Collation (APTC) Through Australian Government For Providing Education Support To Teachers From All The Six Provinces In Vanuatu. These Trainings Were The Most Outstanding Courses That Helps Teachers To Be Able To Work With Students Who Have Special Learning Needs In The Classroom THrough Out Vanuatu.

The Inclusive Education Is NOw Established In Most Schools In Vanuatu But A Lot Of People In The Local Communities Are In Need To Be Aware Of This Program On INclusive Education In Schools.
